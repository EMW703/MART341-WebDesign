Blackened Salmon with Pesto Spinach

What you need

  • Salmon Filet
  • Old Bay Garlic Herb Seasoning
  • Oil with a high smoke point
  • Spinach
  • Pesto
  • Lemon
  • Small potatoes
  • Salt and Pepper

Directions

  1. Heat oven to 400F
  2. Slice potatoes into even bite sized peices, toss with a generous amount of S&P and Olive oil and place in oven.
  3. Pat dry Salmon filet and then scrape the knife on the scale side of the salmon to remove large scales.
  4. Slice half inch slits vertically across the salmon with half inch spaces in between.
  5. Rub the salmon with your garlic herb seasoning, salt and pepper, and a touch of oil.
  6. Toss about a tablespoon of oil on a cast iron pan, and crank the heat up.
  7. When the pan is hot, lay the salmon down on its scale side and cook for 5-6 minutes or until the salmon becomes less translucent.
  8. While the salmon is cookin, toss some spiach and pesto in a sperate pan and stir until spinach begins to wilt. This will be the bed for your salmon.
  9. After 5 minutes or so flip the salmon and allow the other side to cook for about 5 minutes.
  10. Avoid moving the salmon around the pan until you flip if, this will allow the skin to get a better sear.
  11. Remove potatoes from oven, place on a plate net to your pesto spinach.
  12. Before removing salmon from pan, spueeze a bit of lemon over filet
  13. Lastly Pplace your blackened salmon on your bed of spinach
  14. I chose to throw an avocado on the side, goes great with the salmon
  15. Enjoy!
